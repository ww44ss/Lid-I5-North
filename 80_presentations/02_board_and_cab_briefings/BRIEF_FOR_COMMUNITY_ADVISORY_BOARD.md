# Brief for Community Advisory Board

## Purpose

Lid I-5 North is a community-led concept to reconnect neighborhoods divided by Interstate 5 between Wallingford, the University District, Green Lake, Roosevelt, and Ravenna. The Community Advisory Board (CAB), also referred to as the Volunteer Advisory Committee/Council, will provide structured community and stakeholder input during Phase 1 of the feasibility study.

## Core Idea

The project asks whether Seattle can preserve the transportation function of I-5 while creating community value above it: safer crossings, new public space, better walking and biking connections, reduced freeway impacts, stronger local business districts, and a healthier civic landscape for North Seattle. The freeway will continue to serve the region; the land above it can serve the community.

## Strategic Goals

- Reconnect communities divided by I-5.
- Create a high-quality public place.
- Improve environmental and public health outcomes.
- Deliver lasting economic and social benefits.
- Keep the project feasible, equitable, transparent, and broadly supported.

## Priorities for the CAB

- Connectivity and neighborhood reunification.
- Public-space quality and daily usability.
- Health outcomes, including noise, air quality, greenery, and safety.
- Financial viability and realistic implementation pathways.
- Economic activation and local business benefit.
- Community identity, culture, and equitable participation.
- Long-term governance, maintenance, and stewardship.

## Relationship to UDP

UDP is the project grantee and is responsible for grant compliance, fiscal management, consultant contracts, schedule, and final Phase 1 deliverables. The CAB is advisory to UDP staff, the UDP Executive Director, and the project team. The UDP Executive Director or designated staff should participate regularly in CAB meetings, help keep the group aligned with project scope and public obligations, and ensure CAB recommendations are documented and considered. Final decisions on scope, budget, consultant work, partnerships, and deliverables remain with UDP staff under UDP Board oversight.

## Roles and Responsibilities

- Provide input on project goals, priorities, guiding principles, and evaluation criteria.
- Review and comment on key drafts, including engagement plans, consultant work products, conceptual alternatives, and feasibility findings.
- Elevate community concerns, opportunities, risks, and implementation considerations.
- Serve as liaisons and ambassadors to residents, businesses, institutions, and community networks.
- Identify partnerships, resources, and funding or implementation opportunities.
- Support transparent dialogue, disclose conflicts of interest, and work toward clear documented recommendations.
- Provide periodic progress updates to the UDP Board through CAB/VAC co-chairs.

## Early Questions

The next phase should define the study corridor, identify feasible lid or crossing segments, document existing harms, test costs and benefits, and build a funding path. It should compare full lid concepts with near-term improvements such as better sidewalks, protected bike facilities, lighting, crossings, and public-realm upgrades.

## Elevator Pitch

**Lid I-5 North keeps the freeway working for the region while reconnecting the neighborhoods above it. It turns a barrier between Wallingford and the University District into safer crossings, new public space, healthier streets, and a stronger community seam.**
