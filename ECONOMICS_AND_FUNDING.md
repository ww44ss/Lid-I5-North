### Economics, Funding Strategies, and Value Creation

# Purpose

This document evaluates the economic rationale, funding opportunities, and long-term financial implications of the Lid I-5 North project.

The primary questions addressed are:

- What might a freeway lid cost?
- How have similar projects been funded?
- What economic benefits are realistic?
- Can portions of the investment be self-supporting?
- What role can adjacent development play?
- What lessons can be learned from comparable projects?

This document should be treated as a working framework and updated as more detailed engineering and financial analysis becomes available.

---

# Executive Summary

The history of major freeway lid projects suggests several important conclusions.

### Observation 1

Projects are rarely justified solely by transportation benefits.

Successful projects are usually framed as:

- Community reconnection projects
- Public space projects
- Economic development projects
- Environmental improvement projects

---

### Observation 2

Funding is almost always blended.

Successful projects typically combine:

- Federal funding
- State funding
- Local funding
- Philanthropy
- Private development
- Tax revenue capture

No major precedent project relied on a single funding source.

---

### Observation 3

Programming and maintenance are as important as construction.

Several projects demonstrate that operating costs can be as important as capital costs.

---

### Observation 4

Real estate value creation often provides the strongest long-term financial justification.

Many successful projects generate:

- Increased property values
- New development
- Increased tax revenues
- Increased commercial activity

# Case Study: SR 520 Montlake Lid (Seattle)

## Overview

The SR 520 Montlake Project included construction of a three-acre landscaped lid over SR 520 along with transit facilities, pedestrian improvements, and bicycle infrastructure. The project was delivered as part of the larger SR 520 corridor modernization effort. ([WSDOT](https://wsdot.wa.gov/construction-planning/search-projects/sr-520-montlake-project?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## Funding Model

The key lesson from Montlake is that the lid was not funded as a park project.

Instead, it was incorporated into a broader transportation project funded through a combination of state and federal transportation investments. The broader SR 520 replacement program relied on state transportation accounts, federal funding, and bonding mechanisms. ([Federal Highway Administration](https://www.fhwa.dot.gov/ipd/project_profiles/wa_sr520.aspx?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## Lessons for Lid I-5 North

### Positive Lessons

- Transportation funding can support lids.
- Lids can provide mobility benefits.
- Transit integration increases value.
- Bicycle infrastructure strengthens project justification.

### Limitation

Unlike Lid I-5 North, Montlake did not rely on value capture or adjacent development to justify construction.

# Case Study: Presidio Tunnel Tops (San Francisco)

## Overview

Presidio Tunnel Tops created approximately 14 acres of parkland above the Presidio Parkway and has become one of the most successful public-space projects in North America. The park has attracted millions of visitors since opening. ([San Francisco Chronicle](https://www.sfchronicle.com/sf/article/presidio-tunnel-tops-park-overlook-meadow-20759311.php?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## Funding Model

The most important lesson is financial structure.

Construction relied heavily on philanthropy:

- Approximately $98 million raised through a capital campaign.
- Approximately $20 million contributed by the Presidio Trust. ([Golden Gate National Parks Conservancy](https://www.parksconservancy.org/news-coverage/presidio-tunnel-tops-park-sf-has-opening-date-after-118-million-investment?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## Operating Model

The Presidio Trust operates under a unique model.

Revenue sources include:

- Residential leases
- Commercial leases
- Hotels
- Events
- Concessions

These revenue streams support park operations and capital improvements. ([San Francisco Chronicle](https://www.sfchronicle.com/bayarea/article/presidio-trust-defends-report-trump-s-allegations-20205216.php?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## Lessons for Lid I-5 North

### Most Important Lesson

A lid can be partially supported by adjacent revenue-generating real estate.

### Implication

The project should investigate:

- Ground-floor retail
- Event venues
- Commercial space
- Mixed-use development opportunities

# Case Study: Portland Rose Quarter Highway Cap

## Overview

Portland's Rose Quarter project seeks to reconnect the historically Black Albina neighborhood through construction of a highway cover and related improvements. The project includes creation of new land above the freeway. ([Oregon](https://www.oregon.gov/odot/projects/pages/project-details.aspx?project=19071&utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## Economic Lesson

The cap is being discussed not merely as transportation infrastructure but as a mechanism for:

- Community restoration
- Economic development
- Future development opportunities

The newly created land is viewed as an asset capable of supporting future community uses and development. ([Oregon](https://www.oregon.gov/odot/projects/pages/project-details.aspx?project=19071&utm_source=chatgpt.com))

# Real Estate Value Creation Models

## Model A: Park-Only

All lid space devoted to:

- Parks
- Recreation
- Public gathering

### Advantages

- Simplicity
- Public support
- Maximum open space

### Disadvantages

- No direct revenue source

---

## Model B: Park Plus Edge Development

Most space remains public.

Selected perimeter parcels support:

- Cafés
- Restaurants
- Community facilities
- Small retail

### Advantages

- Generates revenue
- Activates park edges
- Improves safety

---

## Model C: Transit-Oriented Development

Integrate:

- Housing
- Offices
- Retail
- Public space

### Advantages

- Strong revenue generation
- Higher tax base
- Increased activity

### Disadvantages

- Greater controversy
- More complex governance

---

## Model D: Air Rights Development

Construct development above portions of the lid.

Examples might include:

- Residential towers
- Office buildings
- Institutional buildings

### Advantages

- Significant value creation

### Disadvantages

- Higher engineering cost
- Potential community opposition

### Mixed Outcomes

Benefits included:

- New businesses

Concerns included:

- Displacement pressures
- Rising property values

## Application to Lid I-5 North

# Preliminary Conclusion

The strongest economic argument for Lid I-5 North is unlikely to be transportation alone.

The strongest justification is likely to be a combination of:

- Community reconnection
- Public-space creation
- Increased property values
- Economic development
- Long-term tax revenue growth

The most promising funding model appears to be a hybrid approach combining transportation funding, federal reconnection grants, local investment, philanthropy, and carefully selected revenue-generating development opportunities adjacent to the lid.

The experience of Montlake, Presidio Tunnel Tops, Portland's Rose Quarter, and transit-oriented development around Othello suggests that the greatest long-term value may come not from the lid itself, but from the new economic activity and community investment that the lid makes possible.
