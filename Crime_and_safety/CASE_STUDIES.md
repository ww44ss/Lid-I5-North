# Case Studies
## Lid I-5 North Project
### Comparative Analysis of Freeway Lid, Highway Cap, and Urban Reconnection Projects

---

# Purpose

This document summarizes relevant precedent projects that may provide lessons for the Lid I-5 North initiative.

The goal is not to identify a single model to replicate. Rather, the purpose is to understand:

- What has worked elsewhere
- What has not worked elsewhere
- Which benefits are realistic
- Which risks should be anticipated
- How other cities approached funding, governance, design, and community engagement

Each case study includes:

- Project overview
- Relevance to Lid I-5 North
- Successes
- Challenges
- Key lessons
- Research needs

---

# How to Use This Document

When evaluating a precedent project, ask:

1. How similar is the surrounding neighborhood?
2. How similar is the freeway condition?
3. How similar is the project scale?
4. How similar is the governance structure?
5. How similar are community expectations?

No precedent is a perfect match.

The value lies in identifying recurring themes.

---

Across multiple projects, several common observations emerge.

# Case Study 1: Klyde Warren Park (Dallas)

## Overview

Klyde Warren Park is one of the most frequently cited freeway lid projects in North America.

The park spans the Woodall Rodgers Freeway in downtown Dallas and reconnects neighborhoods that were previously separated by transportation infrastructure.

Opened: 2012

Approximate Size: 5.2 acres

---

## Why It Matters

This project demonstrates how a freeway cap can become:

- A major public gathering space
- A civic landmark
- A catalyst for economic development

It is often considered the strongest contemporary example of a successful freeway lid.

---

## Conditions Before Construction

Prior conditions included:

- Significant transportation barrier
- Limited pedestrian connectivity
- Underutilized adjacent land
- Fragmented urban fabric

---

## Outcomes Frequently Reported

### Public Space

- High visitation
- Extensive event programming
- Regional destination status

### Connectivity

- Improved walkability
- Strong pedestrian connections

### Economic Activity

- Significant adjacent investment
- Increased development interest
- Increased property values

---

## Criticisms

### Cost

Project costs were substantial.

### Replicability

Some observers argue Dallas may not be representative of all cities.

### Equity

Questions have been raised regarding who benefits from resulting property appreciation.

---

## Lessons for Lid I-5 North

### Strong Lessons

- Programming matters.
- Activity matters.
- Management matters.
- Public-private partnerships can be effective.

### Weak Lessons

- Economic impacts may not translate directly to Seattle.
- Climate and land-use conditions differ.

---

## Additional Research Needed

- Crime outcomes
- Long-term maintenance costs
- Property value impacts
- Public return on investment

# Case Study 2: Rose Kennedy Greenway (Boston)

## Overview

The Greenway was created following removal and burial of the Central Artery.

Unlike a traditional lid, it provides a useful example of urban reconnection following transportation infrastructure transformation.

---

## Why It Matters

It demonstrates:

- Large-scale urban reconnection
- Public-space creation
- Long-term redevelopment effects

---

## Major Achievements

### Public Realm Improvements

- New parks
- Public gathering spaces
- Cultural programming

### Urban Connectivity

- Improved access between neighborhoods
- Improved pedestrian experience

### Civic Identity

The Greenway became an important public asset.

---

## Challenges

### Expectations

Public expectations were extremely high.

### Cost

Costs became politically controversial.

### Governance

Long-term management required dedicated organizational structures.

---

## Lessons for Lid I-5 North

- Long-term governance must be planned early.
- Programming should be considered core infrastructure.
- Public spaces require permanent stewardship.

---

## Additional Research Needed

- Property values
- Maintenance funding

# Case Study 3: Freeway Park (Seattle)

## Overview

Freeway Park is Seattle's most relevant precedent.

Opened in 1976, it was among the earliest examples of a freeway-overbuild project in the United States.

---

## Why It Matters

Unlike many national examples, Freeway Park operates within:

- Seattle's climate
- Seattle's governance structure
- Seattle's cultural context

---

## Major Successes

### Engineering Achievement

Demonstrated technical feasibility.

### Urban Design Innovation

Became internationally recognized for its landscape architecture.

### Neighborhood Connection

Created pedestrian links over I-5.

---

## Major Challenges

### Visibility

Some spaces became difficult to observe.

### Wayfinding

Navigation could be confusing.

### Perceived Safety

Public concerns emerged regarding safety and comfort.

### Maintenance

Long-term upkeep proved challenging.

---

## Important Lesson

The Seattle experience suggests that:

> Connectivity alone is not enough. Successful public space requires visibility, activity, programming, and stewardship.

---

## Implications for Lid I-5 North

The project should avoid creating:

- Hidden areas
- Isolated spaces
- Confusing circulation

The design should prioritize:

- Sightlines
- Activity
- Flexibility
- Visibility

# Preliminary Conclusion

The strongest lesson from comparable projects is that freeway lids succeed when they are viewed as community-building investments rather than engineering projects alone.

The most successful examples combine infrastructure, public space, programming, stewardship, and civic identity into a single long-term vision.

For Lid I-5 North, the evidence suggests that the greatest opportunities lie not merely in covering a freeway, but in reconnecting neighborhoods, creating valued public spaces, and establishing a durable framework for community life above and across the transportation corridor.
